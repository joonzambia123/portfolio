Resume

Greetings! Thanks for dropping by! I’m Joon, a junior majoring in International Studies at Yonsei University. Before discovering product and UX, many a time I’ve felt overwhelmed by the sight of architecturally vast yet ultimately incoherent systems that gave only but the illusion of solvency.

As a self-taught product manager, I’m still struggling to find my own way, but I remain confident in the ability of collaborative design to translate complex systems into beautifully natural interactions.

Currently, I'm serving as an operations clerk at the 89th Artillery Battalion of the Republic of Korea Army, where I lead unit administration, logistics, armory and supply management, human resources, and finances for over 50 combat personnel. Please save me.

Recently, I was a product growth manager at LOVO AI (Skydeck '19), where I worked closely with the founding team to help scale their expanding suite of enterprise voiceover software used by companies like Kakao, LG, Shinhan Bank, Universal Pictures, Lionsgate, and others.

At LOVO, I was the "namer" and one of the team members behind Voice Lab, an advanced voiceover creation tool. Alpha testing happening now!

I’m also the founder of Dongari, a platform where students can pick and choose the collaboration tools they need to build customizable online communities.

You can probably find me arguing with members of my college debate society, sitting in a cafe trying to write something remotely original on Final Draft, or annoying friends with the latest dad joke I found.

Experience

I served as the president of the Yonsei Underwood Union, my university’s varsity parliamentary debate society. At the 2022 Korea Nationals, I achieved a career-high ranking as the #2 ranked debater in South Korea.

I worked on registration and communications as a program associate for the 41st World Universities Debating Championship hosted by Debate Korea.

I was a consultant for the D&B Consulting Group, an online tutoring agency specializing in international admissions. The core team hails from Oxford, Georgetown, Stanford, and other universities.

I led a delegation to MWC as a project manager at TwoNumber, and represented the company at the global venture investment showcase organized by KOTRA.

I previously founded a startup called Palette, a web platform that helped students and organizations discover, register for, and keep a record of their extracurricular activities.
A team comprised of SNU, Sungkyunkwan, and Yonsei student developers and designers produced an MVP, received mentorship from the 1517 Fund, and collaborated with the Yonsei Enterprise Support Foundation on a government grant proposal.

I was a communications intern at DelegatePal, the first open-source research tool for Model UN delegates. The project provided easy access to UN databanks and legislative records, as well as curated educational resources for students.

Personal Notes

I’ve had a nomadic upbringing, growing up in cities like Chicago, Seoul, Qingdao, and Bogota. So I speak 4 languages!

As a self-declared film buff, I love reading and writing screenplays 🎬. I’ve found that there’s something creatively therapeutic about the screenwriting process: it’s a chance to construct characters and dialogue that reflect one's coming of age, and ultimately illuminate a vulnerable but shared humanity that anyone can empathize with. Ask me about the screenplay I’m currently failing to write!

I enjoy playing racket sports like tennis 🎾 and table tennis 🏓. I’m an avid watcher of the ATP and WTA tours, and captained the Appenzeller table tennis team for a while.

I’m a casual online chess player. Feel free to add me on chess.com! I’d be happy to showcase my one-trick Caro Kann. Although you should know that my IQ drops by 70% whenever I play blitz or bullet.

The immortal soundtracks of Forrest Gump, The Perks of Being a Wallflower, Cowboy Bebop, The Intouchables, and Rushmore are the soundtracks to my life 🎸.

My favorite game is League of Legends 🕹, although I’m a lowly Silver player. I’ve been cheering on Faker and SKT T1 since 2016.

I was once invited to TBS eFM’s show “This Morning with Henry Shinn” as a radio panelist. In the 2v2 debate, we discussed delicate issues like female conscription and the modern feminist movement in Korea. Interestingly enough, my teammate (who's male) and I were paired up against a delegation from Ewha Womans University, so the optics of the entire debate were quite unfortunate 🥲. We were both nervous wrecks, trying our best not to say anything stupid on a national broadcast and bring shame to our families. Thankfully, we got out of the studio alive. Pretty thrilling experience!

Randomly, I’ve recently become completely enthralled by Gaelic sean-nós singing.

Currently in the torturous process of trying to learn front end development, data analytics, and Blender animation! Also driving 🚗.

I have a very unique humor code, with favorite jokes including: “What did Sushi A say to Sushi B? Wasabi”, and “You can’t spell Change without Chang” Ha ha ha ha ha ha.
